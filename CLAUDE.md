# Writing Style

## Argument Structure
- Open with a stark declarative statement that delivers the thesis within 30 words. Eliminate extended hooks and scene-setting.
- Introduce core tension early using adversative transitions ("Yet," "But," "Still") within the first five sentences. Compress concessions into clauses rather than expanding them into full paragraphs.
- Ground claims in precise data with exact figures and dates. Named real people serve as typological examples illustrating structural claims, not authorities or historical references.
- Keep prescriptions and recommendations bounded to the specific question at hand. Do not escalate arguments to civilisational stakes or broad philosophical implications.
- Close with one ironic or judicial sentence that crystallises the argument into its sharpest form without summarising what came before.

## Tone
- Judicial, not sermonic. Arguments should read like verdicts, not personal reflections or moral exhortations.
- Stay bounded and specific. Do not expand scope beyond what the argument requires.
- Precise data and statistics legitimise claims. Rely on them rather than appeals to tradition or authority.

## Punctuation
- Never use em dashes (—)
- Never use colons (:)
- Never use semicolons (;)
- Use proper typographic quotation marks: "left" and "right" curly quotes, not straight quotes

## Phrasing
- Never use the negative-positive pair construction (e.g. "it is not X, it is Y"). State the positive directly.
- Use punchy short sentences for emphasis extremely sparingly — once or twice per article at most. Fold emphasis back into the surrounding prose instead.

## Biblical Quotations
- Do not cite the passage reference in the prose. Attribution should be seamless, naming the speaker or author naturally: "David wrote...", "Paul says...", "Jesus tells her..."
- The passage reference follows the quotation in parentheses, linked to the relevant chapter on BibleGateway.com using the NABRE version
- Example format: "My heart is glad, my soul rejoices; my body also dwells secure." ([Psalm 16](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Psalm+16&version=NABRE))
